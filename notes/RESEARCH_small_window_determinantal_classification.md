# Small-Window Determinantal Classification for Realized Prüfer Fibers

## Goal

The note
[RESEARCH_projective_determinantal_fibers.md](/Users/shunhu/Documents/Codes/Lean/notes/RESEARCH_projective_determinantal_fibers.md)
shows that on a bounded-bandwidth window `\mathcal W_{K,L}`, the realized nonunique-fiber locus
is the determinantal rank-drop stratum
\[
\Sigma_1(K,L)=\{[x]:\operatorname{rank}T_x\le N-2\},
\qquad N=\dim \mathcal W_{K,L}.
\]

This note records the first exact classifications of `\Sigma_1(K,L)` on the smallest windows.

The main point is not the low-dimensional examples themselves. The point is that the realized
fiber geometry really does crystallize into explicit projective varieties, and in the mixed
windows it already looks strikingly hypersurface-like.

## 1. Coordinate conventions

Write the bounded-window basis as
\[
(x_0,\dots,x_{N-1})
\]
in the order
\[
J_0,\ \widetilde J_2,\ \widetilde J_4,\dots,\ \widetilde J_1,\ \widetilde J_3,\dots
\]
truncated according to the window `(K,L)`.

Thus:

- on `(K,L)=(1,0)`, the coordinates are `(x_0,x_1,x_2)` attached to
  \[
  (J_0,\widetilde J_2,\widetilde J_1);
  \]
- on `(K,L)=(1,1)`, they are `(x_0,x_1,x_2,x_3)` attached to
  \[
  (J_0,\widetilde J_2,\widetilde J_1,\widetilde J_3);
  \]
- on `(K,L)=(2,1)`, they are `(x_0,x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)` attached to
  \[
  (J_0,\widetilde J_2,\widetilde J_4,\widetilde J_1,\widetilde J_3).
  \]

## 2. The first mixed window `(1,0)`

### Theorem 2.1

On the window `(K,L)=(1,0)`, the realized nonunique-fiber locus is the hyperplane
\[
\Sigma_1(1,0)=\{x_0+x_1=0\}\subset \mathbb P^2.
\]

### Proof

The rank-drop ideal of the determinant matrix `T_x` has Groebner basis
\[
(x_0-x_1)(x_0+x_1),\qquad
x_1(x_0+x_1),\qquad
x_2(x_0+x_1).
\]
Hence its radical is generated by `x_0+x_1`.

## 3. The window `(1,1)`

### Theorem 3.1

On the window `(K,L)=(1,1)`, the realized nonunique-fiber locus is the projective quadric
\[
\Sigma_1(1,1)=\{Q_{1,1}=0\}\subset \mathbb P^3,
\]
where
\[
Q_{1,1}(x)=x_0^2+x_0x_1-2x_2x_3-2x_3^2.
\]

### Proof

The rank-drop ideal has Groebner basis
\[
(x_0-x_1)Q_{1,1},\qquad
x_1Q_{1,1},\qquad
x_2Q_{1,1},\qquad
x_3Q_{1,1}.
\]
Hence its radical is generated by the irreducible quadric `Q_{1,1}`.

## 4. The window `(2,1)`

### Theorem 4.1

On the window `(K,L)=(2,1)`, the realized nonunique-fiber locus is the projective cubic
hypersurface
\[
\Sigma_1(2,1)=\{Q_{2,1}=0\}\subset \mathbb P^4,
\]
where
\[
Q_{2,1}(x)
=
x_0^3+x_0^2x_1-5x_0^2x_2-6x_0x_1x_2+3x_0x_2^2
-2x_0x_3x_4-2x_0x_4^2
+9x_1x_2^2+9x_2^3-6x_2x_3^2-6x_2x_3x_4.
\]

### Proof

The rank-drop Groebner basis is
\[
(x_0-x_1+5x_2)Q_{2,1},\qquad
x_1Q_{2,1},\qquad
x_2Q_{2,1},\qquad
x_3Q_{2,1},\qquad
x_4Q_{2,1}.
\]
Hence the radical is generated by the cubic `Q_{2,1}`.

## 5. The exceptional pure-even window `(2,0)`

The first pure-even mixed window behaves differently.

### Proposition 5.1

On `(K,L)=(2,0)`, the rank-drop ideal is not principal. Its Groebner basis contains several
distinct factors, for example
\[
(x_0-3x_2)(x_0^2+3x_0x_2-x_1^2+9x_2^2),
\qquad
x_3(x_0-x_1)(x_0+x_1),
\]
together with higher mixed monomials.

So `\Sigma_1(2,0)` is reducible and does not collapse to a single hypersurface.

### Interpretation

This is the first sign that windows carrying only one odd mode behave less rigidly than the more
balanced mixed windows.

## 6. A first pattern

The exact calculations suggest the following organizing principle.

### Observation 6.1

For the mixed windows
\[
(K,L)=(1,0),\ (1,1),\ (2,1),
\]
the nonunique realized fiber locus `\Sigma_1(K,L)` is a single projective hypersurface of degree
\[
K+L.
\]

That is:

- degree `1` on `(1,0)`;
- degree `2` on `(1,1)`;
- degree `3` on `(2,1)`.

This may be the first visible trace of a more general projective model-space law for windows
whose parity balance is not too degenerate.

## 7. Research meaning

These small-window computations matter for two reasons.

1. They show that the determinantal reformulation is not merely conceptual; it gives explicit
   projective equations.
2. They indicate that the realized fiber geometry may admit a window-by-window projective
   classification, with balanced mixed windows behaving like single hypersurfaces and more
   degenerate windows breaking into reducible strata.

So the next natural step is:

> determine whether a larger class of mixed bounded windows has hypersurface-type
> nonunique-fiber loci, and identify the degree and geometry of those hypersurfaces.
